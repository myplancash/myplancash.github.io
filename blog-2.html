Why the pursuit of money isn’t bringing you joy

  • Xin Tirol
  • May 26, 2020
  • 12 Comments
Xin
 50     65

The most important question human beings can ask themselves is "What really makes us happy? " We think the things that we want will make us happy, which they tend not to do
The constant striving for wealth and material possessions increasingly could make you unhappy.

Many of us have irrational, magical ideas about what money can do for us, okay, After all, there are bills to pay, but I mean in those things where Dad and Mom work longer hours for more money to get more things.

Research shows that once peoples make more than enough to meet their basic needs, additional money does not reliably promote greater happiness, yet over our choices do not reflect this reality.

The most beautiful things

It is important to remind ourselves that the most beautiful things in life consist of good memories and moments. Furthermore, many people use money as a way of keeping the scoring in the game of life, so it can symbolize economic security, status, power, love, joy and more, and surely many people think that more money would make them a little happier, so we structure our life around some kinds of goals that we think are going to make us happy that are actually false.

So, choosing the right goals is crucial to establishing a meaningful and well-lived life.

“Give your kids what money can never buy—treasured time with you.” — BJ FOSTER

Thus, it bears repeating that money can’t buy things like time, happiness, inner peace, integrity, love, character, health, respect, morals, trust, and dignity. Although money may provide access to the finest earthly pleasures, it may decrease your ability to enjoy the simple joys of everyday life, we tend to define our life on the big moments, our graduation, when we get married, a promotion, or the birth of our children, yet life really happens on everything in between, small simple moments that enrich our lives, moments of joyous laughter are the bricks for a strong family foundation.
